The Air Pollution Crisis

Air pollution is one of the most pressing environmental health challenges of our time. According to the World Health Organization, air pollution kills 7 million people worldwide every year, with 90% of the global population breathing air that exceeds WHO guideline limits.

Major Health Problems

Air pollution doesn't just affect the environment—it directly threatens human health in multiple ways:

Respiratory Diseases

Asthma, COPD, lung cancer, and reduced lung function

Cardiovascular Problems

Heart attacks, strokes, and high blood pressure

Neurological Effects

Cognitive decline, dementia, and developmental issues in children

Pregnancy Complications

Low birth weight, preterm birth, and developmental delays

Environmental Consequences

Air pollution doesn't stop at human health—it fundamentally alters our planet's climate and ecosystems:

  • Climate Change: Greenhouse gases trap heat, accelerating global warming
  • Acid Rain: Sulfur and nitrogen oxides create acidic precipitation
  • Ecosystem Damage: Pollutants harm plant life and biodiversity
  • Reduced Visibility: Smog and haze reduce air quality and visibility
  • Water Contamination: Airborne pollutants settle in water bodies
